# Write Story

Build fiction through seven interconnected layers. Each layer strengthens the
others. Based on narrative science: stories work when every layer serves the
meaning.

## Seven layers

### 1. Meaning
The theme. What is the story really about? Define the controlling idea before writing anything. Theme can be pre-defined ('this story is about loss') OR discovered during writing. Don't force it upfront if it isn't clear.

### 2. Character
People who embody the theme through action. Build from desire (what they want) and need (what they lack). Characters change because the plot forces them to.

### 3. Plot
Events that pressure characters into revealing who they are. Structure: status quo, inciting incident, escalating complications, crisis, climax, resolution.

### 4. Mystery
Questions that pull the reader forward. Plant questions early. Delay answers. Every scene should open or close at least one question.

### 5. World
The setting as a character. Rules, constraints, atmosphere. World-building serves the story. Cut details that do not.

### 6. Relationships
How characters affect each other under pressure. Relationships reveal character faster than internal monologue.

### 7. Prose
The sentence-level craft. Voice, rhythm, imagery. Prose is the last layer, not the first. Get the structure right before polishing sentences.

Layers are interdependent — character informs plot, plot informs mystery, mystery informs relationships. Iterate rather than complete one layer before moving on.

## Workflows

Workflows are independent. Start at any layer or workflow based on your current need — you don't have to go linearly Interview → BuildBible → WriteChapter → Revise.

### Interview
Discover the story through questions before writing.
Steps: ask about theme, characters, world, conflict. Build a shared understanding of the story's DNA.

### BuildBible
Create a story bible: characters, world rules, timeline, themes.
Steps: compile interview answers, fill gaps, create reference docs.

### WriteChapter
Draft a chapter or scene.
Steps: review bible, identify chapter goals (plot, character, mystery), write draft, check all seven layers are served.

### Revise
Improve existing prose.
Steps: identify weakest layer, diagnose specific issues, rewrite with targeted improvements. Layer by layer, not line by line.

## Examples

- "Help me develop a story about [concept]"
- "Interview me about my novel idea"
- "Write chapter 3: [scene description]"
- "Revise this chapter. The pacing feels off."
