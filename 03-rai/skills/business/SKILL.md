# Business

Business content for external audiences. Go-to-market work: pitching, presenting, pricing.

## Routing table

| Task | Sub-skill | File |
|------|-----------|------|
| Sales narrative, pitch deck, battlecard, sales email sequence | Sales | `sales.md` |
| Talk decks, conference talks, internal briefings, investor decks | Presentations | `presentations.md` |
| Pricing pages, packaging, tier copy, anchor pricing | Pricing | `pricing.md` |

## How to use

1. Pick the sub-skill that matches the work.
2. `Read` the file in this directory.
3. Follow that file's process.

## When NOT to use this router

- **Client proposals, RFP responses, scoped pitches** → `/writing/proposals` (moved 2026-05-03 — prose craft layer)
- **Product Requirements Documents** → `/writing/prds` (moved 2026-05-03)
- **Long-form blog essays** → `/writing/blog`
- **Short-form social posts (X, LinkedIn, Substack notes)** → `/writing/social-media`
- **Arabic prose** → `/writing/arabic`

## Why this router stays narrow

`/business` is go-to-market work where prose is one ingredient among many (sales narrative + deck design + pricing strategy). `/writing` owns the prose-craft layer — proposals and PRDs are document compositions before they're business artifacts, so they live there.
