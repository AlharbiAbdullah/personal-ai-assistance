# Proposals

Write a proposal that wins. Differentiated, scoped, priced.

## Voice mandate

Read `references/voice.md` before drafting. Buyers smell AI-written proposals from a paragraph in. The banned-word list, no-em-dash rule, and ownership-voice gate are non-negotiable for this format.

## When to use

- External client or prospect wants scoped work estimated + priced
- Responding to an RFP (Request for Proposal)
- Pitching a consulting engagement, pilot project, or statement of work

## When NOT to use

- Internal feature scoping → `/writing/prds`
- Sales narrative / deck → `/business/sales`
- Pricing page copy → `/business/pricing`
- Pitch deck / investor presentation → `/business/presentations`

## Process

1. **Understand the ask** — request + read the RFP if one exists; extract:
   - Buyer's explicit goals (what they ask for)
   - Buyer's implicit goals (what they REALLY want — ask if unclear)
   - Scope boundaries (what's in, what's out)
   - Evaluation criteria (price, speed, expertise, references)
   - Decision timeline + decision-makers

2. **Qualify** — before writing, confirm you want to win this:
   - Fit with expertise
   - Margin math (time + cost vs. proposed fee)
   - Reference client risk (can you cite the work later?)

3. **Structure** — fill each section tailored to the buyer:
   - **Executive summary** — 1 page max. What + why + how much + when.
   - **Understanding of the problem** — in the buyer's words; shows you listened
   - **Proposed approach** — phased, with decision points
   - **Team** — names, roles, relevant credentials. Real humans who'll do the work.
   - **Timeline** — milestones with dates; ≤3 phases typically
   - **Deliverables** — concrete artifacts the buyer can point at
   - **Pricing** — fixed-fee preferred; hourly if work is truly unbounded
   - **Terms** — IP, NDA, payment schedule, kill switches
   - **Case studies / references** — 1–3 analogous wins
   - **Appendix** — methodology detail, tool choices, risk register

4. **Tailor** — every proposal is custom. Pull boilerplate into templates but never ship unedited.

5. **Review + submit** — double-check buyer name spelling, numbers, dates. Submit via their required channel.

## Anti-patterns

- Generic proposals copy-pasted from other buyers — rejection signal
- Scope creep baked in ("and anything else you need") — erodes margin
- Hourly pricing without cap — buyer fears open-ended exposure
- No case studies — you're asking them to trust you with evidence from others
- Technical jargon in executive summary — the decision-maker is not technical

## Pricing shape

- **Fixed fee** — best for well-scoped work. Risk is on you. Margin protected by scope discipline.
- **Time & materials with cap** — middle ground. Billed hourly, capped at X. Good for ambiguous scope.
- **Retainer** — ongoing engagement, flat monthly. For continuous services.
- **Success-based** — portion of fee tied to outcome. Only if the outcome is measurable and within your control.

## Output

A client-facing PDF or formatted doc. Sections above, tailored voice, no typos, accurate math.

Voice anchor: `~/helm/04-work/client-alpha/ClientAlpha_Data_Lake_Proposal.md` is the long-form template that worked. Match its rhythm: problem in plain language, ASCII diagrams, tables for structured info, no flourish.
