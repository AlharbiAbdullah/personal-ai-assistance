# On Finishing

> Example soul note — reflective writing. These live in `02-ana/soul/` and are personal,
> never published. Use the "Soul Note" template for new ones.

---

For most of my twenties I was a starter. New idea, new repo, new burst of energy — and
then the slow fade somewhere in the unglamorous middle, right before it would have become
real. I had a folder full of 80%-done things and told myself I was prolific.

I wasn't prolific. I was avoidant. The middle of a project is where the learning is, and
it's also where the ego stops getting rewarded. No more "ooh, new." Just the grind of
making the thing actually work.

What changed wasn't motivation. It was a rule: **finish before you start.** One thing
gets to be in progress. It's allowed to be boring. It's allowed to be hard. But it ships
before the next shiny thing gets oxygen.

The first time I held that line through the boring middle and shipped something a stranger
actually used, it rewired me. Finishing is a muscle. I'd just never trained it.

The half-done folder is empty now. That's the whole story of the last few years.
