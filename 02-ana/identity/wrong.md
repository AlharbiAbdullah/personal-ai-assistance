# Wrong

> What I was wrong about. Kept on purpose — changing your mind is the point.

**Last updated:** 2026-01-01

---

> Tracking past mistakes keeps me honest and stops me re-litigating settled questions.

---

## Things I've changed my mind about

**"More tools = more productive."**
Wrong. For years I chased every new framework. The productive move was mastering a small, boring stack and going deep. Novelty is mostly a tax.

**"I'll focus once things calm down."**
Things never calm down. Focus is a decision you make in the noise, not a reward you get after it.

**"Side projects should be ambitious to be worth it."**
Wrong. The ambitious ones died in the planning phase. The small, finished ones taught me more and occasionally found users.

**"Debt is just a tool."**
Technically true, easy to abuse. Carrying a high-interest balance is a guaranteed loss no side investment reliably beats. Paying it down *is* the highest-return move.

**"AI is overhyped for real engineering."**
Half-wrong. The hype is real *and* the substance is real. Dismissing it cost me a year of leverage.

---

## The pattern

Most of my mistakes were the same shape: optimizing for *more* (more tools, more projects, more scope) when the right move was *less, but deeper*.
