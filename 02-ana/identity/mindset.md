# Mindset

> Values, frames, beliefs, principles. The lenses I think through.

**Last updated:** 2026-01-01

---

## Core values

- **Craftsmanship.** Do it well or don't ship it. Quality is a habit, not an event.
- **Honesty.** With myself first. Real numbers, real status, no comforting stories.
- **Family-first.** The work serves the life, not the other way around.
- **Learning.** Stay a student. The day I stop being uncomfortable is the day I stall.

---

## Operating principles

1. **Ship small, ship often.** A thing in users' hands beats a perfect thing in my head.
2. **Depth compounds; breadth doesn't.** Pick a few things; go all the way down.
3. **Make it work, make it right, make it fast — in that order.**
4. **Simplicity is the goal, not a constraint.** If it's complicated, I don't understand it yet.
5. **Finish things.** An open loop costs more than the work to close it.
6. **Spend less than I earn.** Optionality is bought with discipline.

---

## Frames I reach for

- **"Is this reversible?"** Reversible decisions get made fast; one-way doors get slept on.
- **"What would make this easy?"** Before grinding, look for the unlock.
- **"Future me."** Write the code, the note, the plan so the person who reads it in six months thanks me.

---

## Beliefs about work

- Leverage beats hours. Tools, automation, and AI multiply a focused person.
- Boring foundations win. The unglamorous layer is where durable value lives.
- Reputation is the slow compound interest of doing good work in the open.
