# Story

> The longer journey. Where I came from, the turns that mattered.

**Last updated:** 2026-01-01

---

## The short version

Curious kid who liked taking things apart → computer-science degree → a decade of shipping software, mostly learning by building → now a senior engineer trying to turn skill into agency, with a family at home that keeps the whole thing honest.

---

## The turns that mattered

**Learning to finish.** Early on I started ten things for every one I shipped. The habit that changed my career wasn't a language or a framework — it was closing loops. Finishing is a skill, and it's rarer than it should be.

**Going wide, then going deep.** I spent years being a generalist who could touch anything. Useful, but I plateaued. The growth came when I picked a few areas — systems, data, and now AI — and committed to actually understanding them.

**Building in the open.** The first time I shipped something public and a stranger used it, the feedback loop changed how I work. Reputation and skill compound faster in the open.

**Family as the anchor.** Sam and Zoe reset my definition of success. The point of the leverage isn't more output — it's more presence.

---

## What it taught me

- Persistence beats talent that quits.
- The boring middle of a project is where most people drop out; staying in is the edge.
- You can rebuild almost anything except time with people you love.
