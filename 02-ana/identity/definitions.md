# Definitions

> My personal vocabulary — words I use in a specific way, so the assistant means what I mean.

**Last updated:** 2026-01-01

---

## Terms I use precisely

- **"Ship"** — in real users' hands, not "code complete." Demo ≠ shipped.
- **"Deep work"** — a 90-minute-plus block, no notifications, one problem.
- **"Open loop"** — anything started and unfinished that's taking up mental RAM. I close these on purpose.
- **"Leverage"** — output per unit of my time. Tools, automation, and AI are leverage; busywork is the opposite.
- **"Foundation work"** — the unglamorous layer (data, infra, tooling) that other work depends on. My favorite kind.
- **"Future me"** — the person who'll read this code/note/plan in six months. I write for them.
- **"Tuition"** — money I expect to lose while learning (e.g. paper-trading, a failed experiment). Budgeted, not regretted.

---

## Phrases that mean "stop"

- **"Is this reversible?"** — if yes, decide now; if no, slow down.
- **"What would make this easy?"** — look for the unlock before grinding.
- **"Less, but deeper."** — my default tiebreaker when scope creeps.

---

## Why this file exists

When an AI assistant drafts or decides on my behalf, shared vocabulary prevents subtle misreads. This is the glossary for *me*.
